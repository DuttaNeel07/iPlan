About iPLAN

iPLAN is the India Chapter of ACM SIGPLAN the Special Interest Group on Programming Languages. iPLAN serves as the institutional home for India's growing community of programming language researchers, compiler engineers, and students, unifying a rich set of activities under one recognised ACM umbrella.

The chapter emerges from sustained community-building work over the past decade at IIT Bombay and partner institutions, and from the momentum generated by flagship events such as IICT, RHPL at FSTTCS, PLACID, the LLVM Social Seminar Series, Summer/Winter Schools on PL and Compilers, SEGFAULT Hackathon, and the PL & Compilers Devroom at INDIAFOSS.

Mission

Programming languages and compiler technology are foundational to all of computing. iPLAN's mission is to strengthen this ecosystem in India by:

  • Promoting high-quality research in programming languages, compilers, and related formal methods.
  • Creating forums for interaction between academia, industry, and students across India.
  • Enabling regular conferences, workshops, schools, and lecture series so that researchers and practitioners can benefit from the latest advances.
  • Bridging the gap between cutting-edge PL research and industrial compiler practice in India.
  • Fostering an inclusive community that spans IITs, IISc, NITs, and industry R&D labs.

Activities

iPLAN is the institutional roof over the following recurring activities:

IICT - Innovations in Compiler Technology

IICT is iPLAN's flagship two-day workshop, driven jointly by industry and academia. The 2025 edition was an ACM event under iSOFT, sponsored by NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Google, Quadric, and CDAC, and attracted over 380 participants. IICT 2026 is an official ACM iPLAN event.

RHPL – Research Highlights in Programming Languages

RHPL is a workshop co-located with the FSTTCS conference, showcasing high-quality published work as well as mature unpublished research. The 2025 event was spread over three days with blended sessions in the FSTTCS Track B, and there is an ongoing effort to convert it into a dedicated PL track.

PLACID – Programming Languages and Compilers in the Industry Day

PLACID exposes students to challenging opportunities in PL and compiler engineering in India's industry. Participating companies have included AMD, Agrani Labs, Graviton Research, IBM, MathWorks, Microsoft Research, NVIDIA, Quadric, Qualcomm, Saankhya Labs, TCS, and Tejas Networks.

LLVM Social Seminar Series

Monthly seminars many in person in Bangalore, some online actively managed and patronized by industry. Topics have ranged from Static Single Assignment and GPU architectures to PL techniques for FPGA compilation. Community coordination happens through a WhatsApp channel with several hundred members.

Summer / Winter Schools & SEGFAULT Hackathon

ACM Summer and Winter Schools on PL and compiler topics have run almost every year since 2019, targeted at senior undergraduate and postgraduate students. The SEGFAULT hackathon, co-located with IICT, attracted 100+ participants across 47 teams in its inaugural edition, with prizes sponsored by CDAC. Top teams presented their work at IICT.

PL & Compiler Devroom @ INDIAFOSS

The LLVM Social community hosted a half-day miniconference within INDIAFOSS India's largest Free and Open Source Software conference featuring seven talks on PL and compilers and drawing 100+ attendees.
