Conferences & Events

iPLAN organises and supports a set of recurring events focused on programming languages, compilers, and related areas — spanning flagship workshops, research meetings, industry days, hackathons, and lecture series.

Flagship Series

Innovations in Compiler Technology (IICT)

IICT is iPLAN's flagship two-day workshop, driven jointly by industry and academia. It brings together compiler practitioners, PL researchers, and students under one roof. No proceedings are published, enabling open and candid technical exchange. IICT 2025 was an ACM event under iSOFT, sponsored by NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Google, Quadric, and CDAC, with over 380 participants. IICT 2026 is an official ACM iPLAN event.

Visit the IICT website →

Research Highlights in Programming Languages (RHPL)

RHPL is a workshop co-located with the FSTTCS conference. It features presentations of high-quality published work and mature unpublished research, patronized primarily by researchers and students. The 2025 event spanned three days with blended sessions in FSTTCS Track B. There is an active effort to convert it into a dedicated PL track within FSTTCS.

Programming Languages and Compilers in the Industry Day (PLACID)

PLACID is a unique event that exposes students to challenging opportunities in PL and compiler engineering within Indian industry. The 2024 edition served IIT Bombay students; the 2025 edition was opened virtually to external students. Participating companies have included AMD, Agrani Labs, Graviton Research, IBM, MathWorks, Microsoft Research, NVIDIA, Quadric, Qualcomm, Saankhya Labs, TCS, and Tejas Networks.

LLVM Social Seminar Series

Monthly seminars – many held in person in Bangalore, some online – actively managed and patronized by industry. Topics have included Static Single Assignment, Orthogonally Replicated Data Types, Introduction to GPU architectures, and PL techniques for FPGA compilation. Coordinated through a community WhatsApp channel.

ACM Summer & Winter Schools

ACM-branded schools on PL and compiler topics, running almost every year since 2019, targeted at senior undergraduate and postgraduate students.

SEGFAULT Hackathon

Co-located with IICT, SEGFAULT is a compiler and PL-themed hackathon. The inaugural edition attracted 100+ participants across 47 teams, with CDAC-sponsored prizes of ₹75,000, ₹50,000, and ₹25,000. Top six teams presented their work at IICT.

PL & Compiler Devroom @ INDIAFOSS

The LLVM Social community hosted a half-day miniconference within INDIAFOSS – India's largest FOSS conference – featuring seven talks on PL and compilers and drawing 100+ attendees.
